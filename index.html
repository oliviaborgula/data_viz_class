(Text via Britannica) In January 1999, Stewart replaced Craig Kilborn on 'The Daily Show', a parodic news program on the Comedy Central cable network that satirizes media, politics and pop culture. With Stewart as anchor, the show increased its viewership dramatically. .

During the 2000 U.S. presidential campaign, its “Indecision 2000” coverage — featuring Daily Show correspondents reporting from the caucuses and national conventions — became so popular that on election night its viewership rivaled that of some traditional news programs

The host played a role in launching the careers of many prominent comedians and correspondents, including Stephen Colbert, John Oliver, Samantha Bee, and Hasan Minhaj.

He had relatively consistent amount of guests each year until 2015 — his last year hosting until his return in 2924. There was a slight dip in 2007, which also saw special anniversary programs with no guests.

The total number of guests each year stayed consistent until Stewart's final year in 2015

There were about 160 guests each year, ranging from 100 in 2015 to 169 in 2000.
